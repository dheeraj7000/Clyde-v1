# Product: Clyde Economic Simulator

Clyde is a situation-agnostic economic simulator. Users describe any economic event, policy, or shock in natural language. Clyde builds a causal model of affected actors and incentives, runs an ensemble of Monte Carlo simulations, and explains how effects propagate — producing outcome ranges, risk pathways, and intervention points rather than a single forecast.

## Key Concepts

- **Situation-agnostic**: No hardcoded scenario types. Adapts to any economic situation (micro, sectoral, macro, cross-border).
- **Distributional outputs**: Central, optimistic, pessimistic, and tail paths — never a single-point forecast.
- **Evidence-only reporting**: Every claim in a report traces to simulation data. No LLM hallucination in outputs.
- **Branching**: Users can inject new events mid-timeline via natural language ("God's Eye Console") and compare alternate futures.
- **Empirically grounded**: Behavioral parameters come from published research (elasticities, central-bank papers, peer-reviewed studies). All citable.
